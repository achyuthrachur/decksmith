### Color Rules — What to Avoid
- ❌ Do not use secondary colors for text
- ❌ Do not use secondary colors for backgrounds
- ❌ Do not use low-contrast text/background combinations
- ❌ Do not use large areas of black for backgrounds
- ❌ Do not use gradients as backgrounds (SmartPath, data viz, and infographics only)
- ❌ Do not let any secondary color dominate a layout
- ❌ Do not assign specific colors to service lines or departments (unified approach only)

### Color Philosophy
Crowe uses a **unified color approach** — no color is assigned to a specific segment, product, or department. All colors apply across all use cases to maintain cohesion and avoid fragmentation.

## 3. TYPOGRAPHY

### Primary Typeface: Helvetica Now

| Use Case | Font | Style |
|----------|------|-------|
| Headings (H1, H2) | Helvetica Now Display | Bold |
| Subheadings | Helvetica Now Text | Bold |
| Body copy | Helvetica Now Text | Regular |

### System Fallback Fonts (in order of preference)
1. Arial
2. Helvetica
3. Sans-serif (system default)

> Helvetica Now is **not** automatically deployed to all users. Use fallbacks in web/app contexts where the font may not be available. Load via licensed font provider or Adobe Fonts where possible.

### Type Styling Rules

**Casing:**
- Default: Sentence case
- Title case: Attributions and proper nouns only
- ALL CAPS: Section headings and buttons only
- Never: all lowercase

**Punctuation:**
- No punctuation in headlines, section headings, links, or buttons (unless required for comprehension)
- Do not use `&` or `+` in place of "and"
- Do not use the Oxford/serial comma
- Use en dashes (–) for ranges, not hyphens
- No spaces around em dashes
- End punctuation goes inside closing quotation marks

**Spacing:**
- Maintain balanced line height for readability
- Avoid overly tight or overly loose letter spacing
- Never stretch or distort fonts

### Typography Rules — What to Avoid
- ❌ Non-brand-compliant fonts
- ❌ Neglecting line and letter spacing
- ❌ Excessively small font sizes
- ❌ Too many font weights or styles in one layout
- ❌ Stretched or distorted type
- ❌ Text on overly busy or contrasting backgrounds

## 4. DATA VISUALIZATION STANDARDS

### Style Principles
- **Background:** Always Crowe Indigo Dark (`#011E41`) — no exceptions
- **Text:** White only (`#FFFFFF`) for all labels, headings, and annotations
- **Colors:** Use full palette but limit to a small range per chart (keep visuals bold)
- **Gradients:** Subtle, single-use, graduating to transparency only
- **Shapes:** Bold, simple, robust — no fine lines or small elements
- **Typography:** Helvetica Now (same rules as above)
- **Angles:** Acute angles reflecting the SmartPath/Pinnacle motif

### Narrative First
Every data visual must communicate a clear insight. Always highlight the key takeaway — the chart is the vehicle, not the story.

### What to Avoid in Data Viz
- ❌ Any background color other than dark indigo
- ❌ Any text color other than white
- ❌ Overcomplicating charts with unnecessary elements
- ❌ Overusing colors in a single graphic
- ❌ Fine lines or small shapes (illegible on mobile)
- ❌ Deviating from brand color palette

## 5. BRAND VOICE & TONE

### Personality
- Confident, not arrogant
- Contemporary, not trendy
- Trusted, not stiff
- Clear, not oversimplified

### Writing Principles
- **Brevity is key** — concise, direct messaging over lengthy explanation
- **Simplicity speaks louder** — short, powerful statements over elaborate prose
- **Clarity over cleverness** — the message must land immediately
- **Sentence case default** — approachable, not corporate-stiff

### What Crowe Is Not
- Not casual/informal to the point of unprofessionalism
- Not verbose or jargon-heavy
- Not fragmented by department or service line in voice

## 6. LOGO USAGE

### Logo Assets — File Reference

Official logo files live at `client/src/assets/`. Always use these files.
Never construct, generate, or approximate the logo in code.

| File | Use When |
|------|----------|
| `crowe-logo-white-wordmark.png` | Dark or indigo backgrounds — **default for all tool headers** |
| `crowe-logo-full-color.png` | White or light backgrounds only |

### Usage in React
```tsx
// Dark backgrounds (header, dark surfaces)
import croweLogoWhite from '../assets/crowe-logo-white-wordmark.png'
<img src={croweLogoWhite} alt="Crowe" style={{ height: '28px', width: 'auto' }} />

// Light backgrounds
import croweLogoColor from '../assets/crowe-logo-full-color.png'
<img src={croweLogoColor} alt="Crowe" style={{ height: '28px', width: 'auto' }} />
```

### Rules
- Header always uses white wordmark — header background is always indigo dark
- Never use full color logo on indigo or amber backgrounds
- Never use a black logo
- Minimum rendered height: 24px
- Replace with official SVG vector when available from Crowe Adobe Digital Toolkit

## 7. ACCESSIBILITY STANDARDS

- Maintain sufficient contrast between text and background (WCAG AA minimum)
- Do not rely on color alone to convey meaning
- Use alt-text for all images
- Consider font size across device types (mobile-first)
- Use platform built-in accessibility tools (image descriptions, auto-captions) where available

---

## 8. UNIFIED BRAND PHILOSOPHY

Crowe does not assign colors, fonts, or design patterns to specific business units, service lines, or product types. All digital assets — regardless of source — should be immediately recognizable as Crowe through consistent application of this system.

> **The goal:** Every tool, dashboard, report, and communication produced by the AI Innovation Team is instantly recognizable as Crowe, regardless of which team member built it.
